Every child will be allocated their own keyperson who will play a special part in your child's preschool experience. A keyperson has special responsibility for a specific child and his/her family to ensure the child's care and educational needs are met.

Prior to your child attending their settling in session, we offer a home visit when the Preschool Manager or Leader along with the Keyperson will make arrangements with you to meet you at your own home. The Keyperson's aim is to ensure your child is happy and feels secure at Bolton under 5, and to take responsibility to observe, encourage and extend your child's learning experiences.

We will notify you of your Keyperson's name in your welcome pack, and we will endeavour to ensure that they will be their assigned Keyperson throughout his/her time at Bolton under 5s.
However, unfortunately due to the sessional care offered at Bolton under 5s it is not always possible to mirror all of your child's sessions with their Keyperson.  
With this in mind all Practitioners take the time to get to know your child and build a bond to ensure that your child is happy and settled with every member of the team.

Main duties of a Keyperson

  • To provide for the emotional needs of the child, to comfort and reassure
  • To care for the child.
  • To contribute and ensure that our provision takes into account each child's race, culture, religion, language and family values.
  • To liaise with parents/carers
  • To observe the child's progress and with parents/carers permission, keep records of their child's interests and development on the Tapestry system.
  • To meet with the parents/carers at the bi-annual parents evenings and at other times of the year when required.
  • To beable to meet parents/carers at the beginning and end of each session, or liaise with other Practitioners to do so on their behalf.
  • Where applicable liaise with other settings to ensure progress and development of the child.
  • To liaise with the parents/carers and Special Educational Needs Co-ordinator if there are any concerns such as language, hearing or other areas.
    • ​
  • The Keyperson will ensure parents/carers are always contacted about any concerns involving their child.
  • The Keyperson will act sensitively and respect Confidentiality at all times.
